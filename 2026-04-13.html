03 Music Industry Analysis Music
Vinyl Culture
They Cloned Her Voice, Then Claimed Her Songs | AI Music Scams Are Using Copyright Law as a Weapon Against Real Artists
Chinmaya documents how folk musician Murphy Campbell's voice was cloned via AI, with synthetic versions of her songs uploaded to Spotify by an entity called "Timeless Sounds IR" through distributor Vydia. The same distributor then filed copyright claims against Campbell's original YouTube videos, leveraging YouTube's automated Content ID system that assumes the first registrant owns the song. Campbell's case mirrors attacks on Paul Bender, Veronica Swift, and Grace Mitchell—exposing a structural vulnerability where the infrastructure meant to protect artists becomes a weapon when combined with AI voice synthesis. The deeper issue: Alphabet, which owns YouTube and controls the automated enforcement system, is simultaneously investing $175–$185 billion in the AI technology enabling these attacks. A conflict of interest at scale.
